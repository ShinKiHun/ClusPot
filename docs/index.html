Methodology

how we benchmark DFT-referenced single-point evaluation across every cluster ionic step.

Reference data

  • Cluster trajectories — DFT relaxation runs on metal clusters; every ionic step (not only the final geometry) is benchmarked.
  • Bulk references — pulled from the Materials Project. Each MLIP re-relaxes its own bulk to obtain a model-consistent Ebulk/atom.
  • Coverage — monometallic across the periodic table, bimetallic across binary alloy pairs. HEA in preparation.

Evaluation protocol

  • For each DFT trajectory step, the MLIP performs a single-point evaluation on the same nuclear configuration.
  • Energy and forces are compared to DFT directly — no MLIP relaxation, no atom-by-atom bias correction.
  • Configurations whose MLIP energy or forces explode (catastrophic failure) are split out as anomaly and reported separately from the normal regime.

Formation energy

Per-atom formation energy referenced to the model's own bulk:

E_form = ( E_cluster − Σi ni · Ebulk,i(per atom) ) / Natoms
  • Monometallic: ni = N (single element).
  • Bimetallic: weighted by composition (nA, nB).
  • Each MLIP uses its own relaxed bulk energy — keeps the comparison internally consistent and avoids penalizing models for absolute energy offset.

Robustness metrics

  • FwT@ε — fraction of force components within threshold ε (eV/Å).
  • AFwT — area / mean of FwT across the threshold sweep ε ∈ [0, 1] eV/Å. One number summarizing force fidelity.
  • Anomaly rate — share of trajectory steps flagged as catastrophic relative to the full sample set.

Aggregation rules

  • MAE / Anomaly% / AFwT / Time — sample-weighted mean across systems (exact for MAE).
  • RMSE — sample-weighted root-mean-square (exact when underlying errors share the same scale).
  • R² / Pearson / Spearman / cosine — sample-weighted average across (model, dataset). Treat as a summary, not a re-derivation from raw pairs.

Per-system detail

  • The Home leaderboard collapses everything to one number per MLIP — useful for an at-a-glance ranking.
  • For breakdowns by element, pair, or dataset, see the dedicated Monometallic / Bimetallic pages.
  • Each system page exposes per-element heatmaps, Pareto frontiers, FwT curves, and per-model deep dives.
